METHODOLOGY

Sensitivity analysis tells you which input parameters drive outcomes most strongly. For SIR, peak infections are highly sensitive to β near $R_0 = 1$, the threshold above which an outbreak takes off. Far above that threshold, the curves flatten because the outbreak saturates the susceptible pool. Sweeping $\gamma$ has a complementary effect: faster recovery lowers $R_0$ and shrinks the peak.
